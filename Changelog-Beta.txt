== DEPENDENCIES ==
- Now requires Fencer Perk version 1.5.5. Fencer Perk changes:
  - The Hitchance malus from Sword Thrust and Lunge no longer applies to AI characters.
- Now requires MSU version 0.6.0. MSU Changes:
  - Implemented Weapon Type and Damage Type systems for code backend, which will enable more robust, flexible, and frankly new paradigms of BB weapon and skill modding.
  - Skills can now have modular damage types e.g. x% Blunt, y% Piercing on the same skill.
  - Now shows the Damage Type of a skill in its tooltip.
- Now requires WMS version 1.4.2.
  - Moved the Weapon Type system to MSU.

== MAJOR CHANGES ==
- New Perk Group: Entertainer (Class).
- New Perk Group: Scout (Class). Currently only contains Lookout as t1 perk.
- Moved Second Wind from Fast perk group t5 to Resilient perk group t5.
- New Perk: Dynamic Duo, Fast perk group t5. When there is only one ally within 2 tiles and that ally has no other ally within 2 tiles except you, gain the Dynamic Duo effect. Grants 10 Initiative, 10 Resolve and 5% Melee Defense bonus. If the ally also has this perk, these bonuses are doubled, and whenever that ally gets attacked by an enemy in melee, your next melee attack against that enemy gains +10% chance to hit for one turn. Also grants the Rotation skill even if you do not have that perk. When this effect is active, the first Rotation on your turn has its Fatigue Cost halved and if your ally also this perk, it additionally costs no Action Points.
- New Perk: Always an Entertainer. Entertainer perk group t1. Once per day, when you visit a non-military settlement, gain between 20 and 40 gold. For each level this character is above 1, these values are increased by +2. The values are doubled for medium-sized settlements and tripled for large settlements. If multiple characters with this perk are present, only one of them will entertain in a given city on a given day, and the others will have to entertain in other cities.
- New Perk: Paint a Smile. Entertainer perk group t3. Once per day, increase the Mood of the character with the lowest mood in your roster by 1.
- New Perk: Bulwark, Heavy Armor perk group t5. Gain 2% of your combined current head and body armor durability as Resolve. This bonus is doubled against negative morale checks except mental attacaks.
- Removed Through the Ranks and Piercing Shot from the Crossbow perk group t1 and t6 respectively.
- New Perk: Take Aim, Crossbow t1. Requires Crossbow or Firearm. Unlocks the Take Aim skill which applies to your next ranged attack during this turn. For crossbows, this attack ignores the hitchance penalty from obstacles and the shot cannot go astray. For Handgonnes the attack has its Maximum Range increased by 1 and if used at a shorter range, the area of effect is increased by 1 instead.
- New Perk: Primal Fear, Crossbow t6. Ranged attacks that hit the head have a 25% chance to lower the Morale state of the target by 1 level. Has 100% chance with Crossbows.
- Weapon Master now only provides the mastery bonuses of the perks available in this character's perk tree. Now also requires at least one of the following masteries to be picked before it provides its bonuses: Axe, Cleaver, Dagger, Hammer, Mace, Spear, Sword.
- Double Strike now only works with Melee skills.
- Relax reworked: Now doubles the Fatigue Recovery of the target for their next turn. Cannot be used on the same target two turns in a row. Cannot be used when engaged in Melee.
- Hip Shooter tweaked: Now reduces the Action Point cost of Quick Shot by 1 but each subsequent Quick Shot in this turn builds 10% more Fatigue.
- Entrenched tweaked: Now grants +5 Ranged Skill, Ranged Defense, and Resolve as long as you are adjacent to an ally wielding a melee weapon who is not engaged in melee. The bonuses increase by +1 every turn up to a maximum of +10 as long as you continue to start your turn adjacent to any ally wielding a melee weapon who is not engaged in melee.
- From All Sides tweaked: Now reduces the target's Melee Defense and Ranged Defense by a stacking -3 per hit. Double the effect if it hits the head. Missed attacks reduce the target's defenses by -1 instead.
- Back to Basics reworked: Gain a bonus to key combat attributes depending on how far their Base Values are below a certain thershold. These attributes are increased by a percentage equal to the number of points they are below the threshold. The threshold for Melee Skill and Ranged Skill is 70, and for Melee Defense, Ranged Defense, and Resolve is 40. Also grants +10% Training in camp.
- Removed Man of Steel from Heavy Armor perk group t7. The perk still remains on AI enemies.
- Hybridization tweaked: The Melee Skill bonus is provided at all times, but the Ranged Skill bonus now only works when wielding a Throwing Weapon.

== MAJOR CHANGES ==
- New Perk: "Weapon Master", One-Handed Weapons perk group t7. Grants mastery with all One-Handed Melee weapons and allows swapping one One-Handed weapon for another once per turn for no Action Point cost. Does not stack with Quick Hands.
- New Perk: "Pattern Recognition", Soldier perk group t3. Every attack from or against a target grants a stacking +3 Melee Skill and +3 Melee Defense against that target for the remainder of the combat.
- New Perk: "Bully", Raider perk group t6. Melee Damage is increased by 10% per level of Morale difference between you and your target, as long as your Morale is higher than the target's.
- Reworked Heightened Reflexes (Sword perk group t3). This perk will be renamed in a future build. Now grants 25% of Base Melee Defense as Initiative and percentage Fatigue Cost Reduction on weapon skills, when your armor fatigue penalty is -35. For every point of deviation from -35 the bonuses increase or decrease by 1%. Armor fatigue above -35 increases the Fatigue Cost reduction bonus but reduces the Initiative bonus, and armor fatigue lower than -35 increases the Initiative bonus but reduces the Fatigue Cost reduction bonus. The bonuses can never be negative and never more than 50% of Base Melee Defense.
- Removed Feint from Sword perk group t2.
- Moved Exploit Opening from One-Handed Weapons perk group t5 to Sword perk group t2. In addition to the previous effects, it now adds Riposte to the southern curved swords.
- In addition to its previous effects, Sword Mastery now reduces the threshold to inflict injury by 25% when wielding a southern curved sword.
- Heavy Projectiles reworked: Now requires Sling. Ranged attacks from Slings that hit the head now apply Stunned and that hit the body apply Dazed. If the target is immune to Stun, then hits to the head will apply Dazed and hits to the body will apply Baffled.
- Close Combat Archer: Now also works with Blunt type throwing weapons (e.g. Bolas). With this perk, at 2 tiles, such weapons now have a 100% chance to Stagger upon hit and if the target is already staggered, then stun. The chance is halved at 3 tiles, and is 0 at longer distances.
- The Indomitable effect now prevents you from being Culled.
- The free attack from Flail Spinner now does 50% reduced damage.
- Quick Hands no longer works when swapping one Two-Handed weapon with another Two-Handed weapon.
- Offhand Training, in addition to the previous effects, now also allows you to swap tools in your offhand for free once per turn. Also allows swapping between tool and buckler/parrying dagger. Does not stack with Quick Hands.
- Moved Offhand Training from t7 to t5.
- Unstoppable now only gains stacks from attacks during the attacker's turn. Can still lose stacks by missing attacks when it is not your turn. Hitting targets at 2 tiles now requires two hits to get 1 stack. The two hits do NOT have to be in a single attack. Getting hit now removes half the stacks instead of all of them.
- Perfect Focus: No longer usable when Inspired. Now gives -10% damage and +10% fatigue built for every target hit or miss under the effects of Perfect Focus. Effectively, this means that skills which can be used more times e.g. Quick Shot with Hip Shooter, as well as AOE skills are less overpowered with Perfect Focus, whereas skills which are still usable 2-4 times don't suffer that much.
- Mark Target now costs 7 AP.
- The Professional perk now provides Shield Expert and Weapon Master instead of Duelist and Exploit Opening.
- Buffed the Shield Damage from Throwing Spear to 52. Using it with the Throwing Mastery perk now ignores the target's shield damage reduction from the Shield Mastery perk. Effectively, Throwing Spears will now almost always destroy wooden shields in one hit.
- Overhauled many origin Commander backgrounds to now have a more sensible number of perks in their perk tree.

- Removed Unstoppable Force from t7 Heavy Armor perk group.
- New t7 Heavy Armor perk: "Man of Steel". Reduces armor penetrating damage to Hitpoints received from ranged attacks by up to 30%. The bonus increases exponentially with total head and body armor of higher maximum durability.
- Reworked "Freedom of Movement": Now provides 5 + 10% of your Maximum Fatigue after gear as a constant bonus to Melee Skill and Melee Defense. The bonus decreases exponentially when wearing armor heavier than -15 fatigue.
- Moved Freedom of Movement to t5 of the Light Armor perk group.
- Moved Strength in Numbers from Militia perk group t7 to t2.
- Reduced the probability of the Spear, Sword, Crossbow, and Staff perk groups overall, to allow for more variety in recruits.
- Increased the chances of recruits with the Hunter perk group to also have the Dagger and Devious perk groups.
- Increased the chances of recruits with the Apothecary perk group to also have the Calm perk group.

== Balance ==
- Removed the "Vengeance" perk from all remaining enemies who still had that perk. No enemies have this perk now.
- Removed several accuracy and damage perks from a lot of ranged enemies. This will hopefully make them feel less obnoxious.
- Added "Man of Steel" to some enemies.
- Removed the "Alert" perk from goblin fighters.

== Fixes ==
- Removed Marksmanship and Berserk from goblin fighters. These perks were not intended on them.
- Made it clear in the tooltip of Promised Potential that a successful Realized Potential also unlocks new perk groups.
- Fixed Cull always considering every hit a headshot for its killing threshold calculation when using the Split Man skill of 2H Axes.
- Fixed a typo in the Dismantled armor effect tooltip.
- Fixed a bug in the Dismantled armor effect which may have prevented it from receiving the increased damage.

== Perks ==
- Follow Up skill reworked (due to AI crashing issues): Until your next turn, any enemy in your attack range who gets hit by an ally adds 1 charge to this effect. During your next turn, you can use your primary attack skill freely for as many times as you have charges. These free attacks do -50% damage and an additional -10% for each next attack. Once all charges are used up the effect expires and you return back to the usual costs and damage of your attack. If at any point, you move from your position, or are engaged in Melee, this effect ends immediately and all charges are lost.
- Sanguinary tweaked: In addition to the previous effects, attacking bleeding targets improves this character's morale by 1 level and inflicting a fatality immediately sets the Morale to Confident.
- Deep Cuts tweaked: Every second attack against the same target applies an additional stack of bleeding of 5 damage. This damage is increased to 10 if any of these two attacks inflicted an injury.
- Bloodlust tweaked: Every attack against a bleeding target reduces current Fatigue by 5% per stack of bleeding on the target, and increases Fatigue Recovery by +1 per stack of Bleeding on the target.
- Improved the tooltip of the Survival Instinct effect. It now shows the number of stacks in the effect's name.

== Fixes ==
- Fixed Flail Spinner causing a chain reaction with 3-headed Flails. The skill has been refactored in code and now works with specific skills only i.e. the Flail, Lash, Cascade, Hail, and Pound skills.
- Fixed Follow Up sometimes causing issues with vanilla AI evaluation when it procced and killed a target. The perk has been reworked to allow this fix as mentioned above.
